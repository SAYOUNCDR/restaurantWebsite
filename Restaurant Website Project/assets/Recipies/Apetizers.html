Corn Pakora

Ingridiants :

  • 1 cup sweet corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 2-3 tablespoons rice flour (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep fryinh4

Instructions :

1. If using fresh corn, remove the kernels from the cob. If using frozen corn, thaw and drain excess moisture.

2. In a mixing bowl, combine the sweet corn kernels, gram flour, rice flour (if using), chopped onion (if using), green chili, red chili powder, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, garam masala (if using), asafoetida, chopped coriander leaves, and salt.

3. Gradually add a little water to the mixture and mix well to form a thick batter. The batter should coat the corn kernels evenly.

4. Heat oil for deep frying in a pan or kadai over medium heat.

5. Once the oil is hot, take small portions of the batter using a spoon or your hands and carefully drop them into the hot oil. Fry the pakoras in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.

6. Fry the pakoras until they turn golden brown and crispy, flipping them occasionally for even frying. It usually takes around 4-5 minutes for each batch.

7. Once done, remove the pakoras using a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.

8. Serve hot corn pakoras with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or tomato ketchup as a delicious snack or appetizer.

Remember, adjust the spices according to your taste preferences. Enjoy your crispy and flavorful corn pakoras!

Masala Vada

Ingridiants :

  • 1 cup chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 2-3 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 inch ginger, chopped
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic (optional)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Curry leaves (a handful), chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep fryinh4

Instructions :

1. Wash the chana dal thoroughly and soak it in water for about 2-3 hours. Drain the water completely before grinding.

2. In a food processor or a blender, coarsely grind the soaked chana dal along with dried red chilies, ginger, and garlic (if using). The texture should be coarse, not too smooth.

3. Transfer the ground dal mixture to a mixing bowl. Add chopped onions, green chilies, coriander leaves, fennel seeds, cumin seeds, chopped curry leaves, asafoetida, and salt.

4. Mix all the ingredients well to ensure even distribution of spices and flavors.

5. Heat oil for deep frying in a kadai or frying pan over medium heat.

6. Meanwhile, take a small portion of the mixture and shape it into a ball. Flatten the ball slightly to form a patty shape.

7. Gently slide the shaped vadas into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them in batches.

8. Fry the vadas until they turn golden brown and crispy on both sides, flipping them occasionally for even frying. It usually takes around 4-5 minutes for each batch.

9. Once done, remove the vadas using a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.

10. Serve hot masala vadas with coconut chutney, mint chutney, or tomato ketchup as a delightful snack or as part of a meal.

Enjoy the crispy, aromatic, and flavorful masala vadas!
